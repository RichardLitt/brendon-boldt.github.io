Brendon Boldt

I am a Master of Language Technology student at Carnegie Mellon University's Language Technology Institute working with David Mortensen. My primary interest is artificial emergent languages from the perspectives of machine learning, natural language processing, information, linguistics, and philosophy. Phrased as a series of questions,

  • What kind of language can machine learning-equipped agents invent in order to cooperate on a task?
  • What attributes in the environment correspond to attributes in the emergent language?
  • Instead of starting with a fully-formed language and building up an agent's proficiency in it, can we build up the agent's proficiency as the language is developed from scratch?
  • What even is an interesting question in emergent language? We cannot wantonly throw together experiments and expect knowledge to come out.
  • What are the most productive research directions for reaching a working theory and practice of emergent language?
